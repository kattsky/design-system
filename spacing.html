Spacing

Spacing plays a crucial role in visual design as it provides structure, hierarchy, and readability to content. It can be divided into two categories: margin and padding.

To maintain consistency in spacing throughout the design, rems are used as the unit of measurement for spacing. It includes small increments of 4px.

Below are some examples of spacing in design:

Margin

Margins define the space around the outer edges of an element, establishing its relationship with neighboring elements and the overall layout. Key considerations for margin usage include: consistency, hierarchy, white space, and responsive design.

Padding

Padding refers to the space between the content of an element and its border, providing breathing room and enhancing visual appeal. Important considerations for padding usage include: comfortable spacing, hierarchy emphasis, consistency, and accessibility.
